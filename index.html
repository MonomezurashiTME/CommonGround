01 — The Problem

Empty stores,
hungry consumers.

Independent makers are talented, prolific, and invisible. Traditional retail requires volume and margin most can't sustain. Markets are inconsistent. Online channels are algorithmically brutal. The infrastructure works against the SME.

Wholesale is out of reach
Traditional wholesale demands minimum order quantities and margins that most independent makers simply can't meet at small scale.
Markets aren't a business model
Weekend markets require physical presence every week. They're inconsistent, exhausting, and offer no path to stable retail income.
Online channels are saturated
Etsy, Instagram, and direct-to-consumer platforms reward advertising spend over craft quality. Discovery is broken for the independent maker.

03 — The Concept

Housemates,
for businesses.

Common Ground is a curated physical retail space where independent Australian makers share a storefront, costs, and a collective brand identity. Each maker keeps their story — we provide the shelf, the foot traffic, and the editorial stamp of quality.

Curated entry
Makers apply and are selected on quality, craft, and category fit. The curation is the product — it's what makes the space worth walking into.
Shared economics
Monthly licence fee plus commission on sales. No upfront wholesale risk. Turning inclines into staircases — the prohibitive cost of an entire store divided into something manageable.
Permanent presence
A staffed, permanent retail location. Not a pop-up. Not a market stall. A destination that builds consumer habit and return visits — without requiring makers to be present.
Collective identity
The Common Ground brand does the marketing heavy lifting. Every campaign lifts all makers. The whole is greater than the sum of its parts.

05 — The Tension

Why hasn't it
already been done?

Challenge
Rent viability
Quality retail locations carry high rents. The licence + commission model must cover it or the business doesn't work.
Response
Regional and suburban centres offer viable rents at meaningful foot traffic. Location selection is a deliberate strategic trade-off, not a compromise.
Challenge
Maker churn
If makers leave or underperform, curation quality and revenue base erode simultaneously — a compounding risk.
Response
A waitlist model and active vendor pipeline ensures gaps are filled quickly. Curation standards also improve as patterns become clear.
Challenge
Brand dilution
Without rigorous standards, the collective becomes just another gift store. The brand is only as good as the worst product on the shelf.
Response
Curation standards enforced consistently and with editorial courage. This is a cultural commitment, not just a commercial one.
